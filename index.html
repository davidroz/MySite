Questions & Answers

Q1:   What are the differences between web1, web2 and web3?

Web1:
Simply an information portal where users passively receive information without the ability to post any feedback as comments and reviews.
This version is very limited in interaction between sites and web-users.
It is often called as the "readable" phrase of the web with flat data.

Web2:
Unlike Web1, Web2 facilitates interaction between web users and sites, so it allows users to interact more freely with each other.
This version encourages participation, collaboration, and information sharing.
It is often called as the “writable” phrase of the web world with interactive data.
Examples of Web 2.0 applications are Youtube, Facebook, and so on.

Web3:
Web3 is a semantic web which refers to the future. In Web3, computers can interpret information like humans and intelligently generate and distribute useful content tailored to the needs of users. One example of Web 3.0 is Tivo, a digital video recorder. Its recording program can search the web and read what it finds to you based on your preferences.
It is often called the “executable” phrase of web world with dynamic applications, interactive services, and “machine-to-machine” interaction.



Q2:   Explain these differences using the use-case of the query "Medications Side effects":


Web1:
We got sites with flat information regarding the side-effects of the medical, similar to the medical prospect comes with it.

Web2:
We got many sites contain information about the side-effects, some of the sites includes comments or recommendation of doctors regarding the medical and its side effects.
Some includes former conversation among web-users which have experience using those medications.
More options we expect to appear are: upload personal question or moving to another relevant site

Web3:
We got most relevant sites to our user-profile, in this case we expect to find the conversation or the information for the user age, gender, medical-status(pregnancy, etc.)
